Story

Background

I'd previously made another card game for my friends ("The Muffin Game")
The game came with 2 key problems: The deck was way too big, and the game was too complex
To solve this, I planned to make the game around 54 cards and the base mechanic of "actions then draw"
I also knew this game was to be designed for my friends to enjoy, so should be a party-style game

In order to make the initial draft, I first made sure I had a fun card game before adding any art

Once the initial draft was complete, it came time to add some theming to give the deck character.
The mechanic of drawing cards to reach a final high value card made me think of a mine

Initial Version

This first version had no art yet, and was just printed cards packed into sleeves with pokemon cards.

After playing this version with my friends, it was clear that pretty much all the mechanics were right.
There were just a few issues: The point values were too high, and the Coerce card was a pointless inclusion.

Balancing Update

After updating the cards from this feedback, it was clear that the game itself was ready.

The game can be exploited, but that depends on the group you play with - good for a party game.

Art Update

At this stage, it came time for the deck to get some art.

Initially, the art was going to be vector art of human characters.
I then realised I have no art experience, and the little friends project did not warrant learning to draw.
As a result, the design pivotted to a simpler 8-bit style which I am more comfortable with.
This new paradigm made it difficult to have expressive humans, and thus the titular slime was born.

Distribution Notes

I'd now achieved what I set out to do: make a fun card game for my friends.
So I then thought, why not sell it? Worst case nothing happens, and best case I make some money.

Skills developed/shown

Taking Feedback

  • Game was developed for friends with the express intent of being fun
  • One big criticism was due to how high the scores were, it made the two player experience dreadful
  • Showing the game to other friends led to a Print-N-Play version
  • Personal feedback in knowing what I was able to do

Game Design

  • I made a game - pretty clear evidence
  • Brand management - the game took heavy inspiration from Exploding Kittens to make the template
  • (Anecdotal Evidence) My friends enjoyed playing the game daily, so I must've done good

Business Management

  • Having to think about HOW I was selling the game (Where, What price?, What audience?)
  • Optimizing cost for profit per item and overall sales
  • By restricting myself to 54 cards I trade a more chaotic game for a more cost effective one
